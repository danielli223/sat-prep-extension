# Project Brief — Free SAT App as an Audience Wedge

*Last updated: 2026-06-13*

## One-liner
A free SAT prep web app used as a loss-leader to build a large audience of college-bound teens, to be monetized later by selling adjacent products to their parents.

## The idea
A solo / indie builder (side project, minimal budget, no team) ships a **free SAT prep web app** for US high schoolers. The entry hook is a **free question bank**. The SAT app is not monetized directly — it exists to attract a large, high-intent audience.

## Core bet (the thesis)
*More free users now → monetize them later by selling other useful products to the same group.* The real asset is intended to be the **audience and the owned distribution channel**, not the SAT app itself.

## Growth & monetization plan
- **Phase 1:** Free SAT app → build audience.
- **Phase 2 (expansion):** Add more free apps (e.g., AP exam prep) to widen the audience.
- **Phase 3 (monetization):** Sell adjacent **"student tools" (specific products TBD)** primarily to **parents** (the wallet).

## Constraints
- Solo builder, minimal budget, no team.
- No direct revenue until the (currently undefined) monetization product lands.

## Key risks surfaced in evaluation
- **Undefined Phase 2** — the product that actually makes money hasn't been named or tested (the fatal flaw).
- **Commoditized wedge** — free official prep already exists (Khan + Bluebook + Gemini/Princeton Review).
- **User ≠ buyer** — users are teens; the buyer is parents.
- **Leaky-bucket audience** — ~100% turnover every ~2 years; SEO/brand compounds, the user list does not.
